About Me

I am an Integrated MS/PhD student in Computer Science at Theory of Computation (ToC) Lab, Yonsei University. My research focuses on program analysis and code intelligence, particularly in the areas of time complexity prediction, test case generation, and multi-agent debate frameworks. I explore how large language models (LLMs) can reason about algorithmic properties and generate specification-aware test suites. I have proposed novel methods that integrate chain-of-thought prompting, reinforcement learning, and self-play mechanisms to improve the reliability and coverage of generated test cases.

Research

  • Time complexity prediction and formal analysis of programs
  • Specification-driven test case generation using large language models
  • Multi-agent debate frameworks for reasoning and validation
